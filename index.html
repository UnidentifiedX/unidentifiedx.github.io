My Projects

Always tinkering.

Reinforcement Learning Model for the Tracking of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

The prevalence of low-cost drones suggests a need to track them to prevent misuse. Traditionally, PID controllers are used for tracking target drones, albeit with major limitations. Therefore, the efficacy of a PPO-based agent was explored, with results showing consistent major improvements over PID controllers. This suggests that PPO controllers may serve as a promising alternative to PID controllers.

Control Systems Artificial Intelligence Reinforcement Learning C#
2024

Synthesis and Properties of S. Cerevisiae-based Bionanocomposites

Heavy metal ions, dyes and oil are major water pollutants that harm human and ecological health. Magnetite-coated S. Cerevisiae was synthesized and its adsorbing capabilities evaluated. Overall, results demonstrated that depending on the nature of the pollutant, this yeast-based bionanocomposite demonstrated exceptional adsorption capabilities which demand further exploration.

Materials Science Biochemistry Environmental Chemistry
2024

Optimised Numerical Methods for the Computation of Particles in Fusion Hotspots and Particle Interactions in Magnetised Liner Inertial Fusion (MagLIF)

The Barnes-Hut algorithm was initially created to analytically solve n-body gravitational systems. However, it was adapted to solve particle interactions in plasma hotspots as an alternative to current particle-in-cell (PIC) simulation methods. Simulations demonstrated expected behaviour such as coulomb explosion, while incorporating necessary features such as energy conservation.

Nuclear Fusion Numerical Analysis Plasma Physics C++
2023

Synthesis and Properties of Chitin-derived Hydrogels

Hydrogel was synthesised from chitin, which was extracted from crab shells. This chitin-based hydrogel was used to extract heavy metal ions and dyes, which are common wastewater contaminants. Results show promising adsorption capabilities for certain contaminants, especially when solution pH was adjusted.
